Project description: The goal of this project was to study the non-equilibrium dynamics of quantum materials such as high Tc superconductors, using mid-infrared pump pulses and time-resolved second harmonic generation probe pulses. To do so, I used an advanced table-top optical setup for the generation of pump pulses in the highly unconventional mid-infrared frequencyrange. This setup,which is driven by a conventional Ti:Sapphire laser-amplifier, utilized a cascade of nonlinear optical processes produding white light generation, optical parametric amplification and difference frequency generation to generate the intense pulses which are used to excite the sample. In order to measure the state of the quantum material after excitation, I have deployed various forms of non-equilibrium spectroscopy, including time-resolved second harmonic generation, time-resolved polarization rotation spectroscopy. In addition, I developed a new form of multidimensional spectroscopy which combines mid-infrared and near-infrared pulse sequences in order to overcome the limitations of conventional pump-probe experiments. I conducted many of these experiments at very low temperatures using cryogenic systems based onliquid helium and nitrogen.

In this project, the goal was to synthesize a nanostructured thin film composed of three metallic elements- Ni, Mn, and Sn- with precisely controlled compositional ratios. These nanostructures have the potential to function as magnetic shape memory alloys, a class of materials capable of recovering their original shape under the application of a magnetic field.

For the growth process, I employed the electrodeposition technique to fabricate thin films from a heated solution containing a mixture of dissolved Ni, Mn, and Sn precursors. Following deposition, I performed cyclic and linear sweep voltammetry to investigate the electrochemical kinetics of the solution. The chemical composition of the films was characterized using Energy-Dispersive Spectroscopy (EDS), while their surface morphology was examined using Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) and Field Emission Scanning Electron Microscopy (FE-SEM). Finally, Grazing Incidence X-ray Diffraction (GI-XRD) was used to determine the precise crystal structure of the films.
