About Me

I was born and raised in one of the most historically rich cities in the world, Isfahan, in the heart of Iran. From a very young age, around six or seven, I fell in love with the magic of the stars in the night sky, much like many other children.

What began as childhood curiosity, however, gradually developed into a clear and lasting ambition. I pursued this dream with patience and determination, completing high school in mathematics and physics with a final grade of XX, ranking among the top students of my year.

I went on to earn my Bachelor’s degree in Physics at the University of Isfahan between 2010 and 2014. After graduating in my hometown, I felt ready to embrace a new challenge and slightly redirect my academic path.

I therefore took a one-year gap to prepare for the national entrance examination for a Master’s program in Nanotechnology. The year 2015 proved to be a pivotal period in both my personal and academic development, as I was accepted into Tarbiat Modares University in Tehran.

In 2018, I was accepted into a PhD program at the Max Planck Institute for the Structure and Dynamics of Matter. Due to visa constraints, my move to Germany was delayed until November 2019, but I ultimately joined the research group led by Andrea Cavalleri where I became deeply immersed in the fields of ultrafast lasers and superconductivity.

In May 2025, I successfully defended my PhD thesis with the highest possible grade (1.0). I subsequently continued in the same group as a postdoctoral researcher until September 2025. At that point, I recognized that it was time to move forward, explore new directions, and seek the next stage of my professional journey.
